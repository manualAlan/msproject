\chapter{Introduction}

\section{Motivation: deterministic phase-coherent control in trapped-ion experiments}
Trapped-ion quantum information processing depends on coherent manipulations of qubit states using
electromagnetic control fields engineered in time, frequency, amplitude, and phase.
In practice, high-fidelity operations require (i) deterministic timing to align control actions to the experiment
timeline, and (ii) stable phase relationships between channels that drive optical modulators and microwave/RF
hardware.

This thesis focuses on the signal-generation and timing-control layer required to support such experiments,
with emphasis on ARTIQ’s real-time execution model and the Phaser AWG module.

\section{Contributions}
This master’s project contributes an implementation and validation framework for Phaser-based RF control.
Unlike a purely descriptive hardware report, the emphasis is on the system-level mapping from experimental
requirements to timing semantics, device configuration, and validation.
